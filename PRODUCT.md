# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indonesian university students looking to find project teammates, collaborate on academic/non-academic projects, and build their portfolio. They're mobile-first, browsing between classes or during downtime. Bahasa Indonesia is the primary language.

## Product Purpose

Rembugan is a student collaboration platform — "LinkedIn for Students." It exists to connect students across campuses for project matchmaking, team collaboration, and portfolio building. Success looks like students consistently finding teammates and shipping projects together.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious · Collaborative · Modern

The app should feel forward-looking and capable without being sterile. It's a tool made for students by people who understand students — professional enough to trust, warm enough to use daily.

## Anti-references

- **Too corporate / LinkedIn-clone**: Sterile, over-professional corporate aesthetic. This is a student platform, not a recruiting tool.
- **Too gamified / childish**: Cartoonish elements, excessive badges, gamification that undermines the professional ambition.
- **Too generic / SaaS-template**: Purple-blue gradient heroes, identical card grids, stock illustrations, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section. The saturated AI SaaS look.

Find the student-authentic middle ground: ambitious but not corporate, polished but not sterile, modern but not trendy.

## Design Principles

1. **Student-native, not corporate.** The app should feel like it belongs to students — approachable, energetic, direct. Design decisions should pass the test: "would this feel at home in a campus app?"

2. **Substance over decoration.** Avoid tropes (gradient text, glassmorphism, side-stripe borders, identical card grids) in favor of purposeful, minimal design. Every element earns its place.

3. **Flow-forward, not page-bound.** The primary job is finding and joining projects. Navigation, information architecture, and interaction design should optimize for that path, not for feature parity.

4. **Trust through clarity.** Actions should be predictable. Forms should give instant feedback. Errors should be helpful, not cryptic. No mystery meat navigation.

5. **Inclusive by default.** WCAG AA minimum from day one — contrast, keyboard navigation, reduced motion support. Accessibility is not a polish step.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Contrast ratios ≥4.5:1 for body text, ≥3:1 for large text. Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard navigable throughout.
